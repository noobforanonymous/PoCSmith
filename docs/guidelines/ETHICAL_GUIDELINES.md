## Responsible Use

### Before Using PoCSmith

1. **Obtain Written Permission**
   - Never test systems you do not own without explicit authorization
   - Get written permission from system owners
   - Document scope and boundaries of testing

2. **Know the Law**
   - Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA) - USA
   - Computer Misuse Act - UK
   - Local cybersecurity laws in your jurisdiction
   - Unauthorized access is illegal in most countries

3. **Follow Responsible Disclosure**
   - Report vulnerabilities to vendors privately
   - Allow reasonable time for patches
   - Coordinate public disclosure responsibly

### During Security Testing

1. **Stay Within Scope**
   - Only test systems explicitly authorized
   - Do not exceed agreed-upon testing boundaries
   - Stop immediately if you encounter unexpected access

2. **Minimize Impact**
   - Avoid denial of service attacks
   - Do not modify or delete production data
   - Test during agreed-upon time windows

3. **Document Everything**
   - Keep detailed logs of all testing activities
   - Document vulnerabilities discovered
   - Note exploitation steps for reporting

### After Discovery

1. **Report Responsibly**
   - Contact vendor security teams
   - Provide clear reproduction steps
   - Suggest remediation if possible

2. **Protect Information**
   - Do not publicly disclose before patches available
   - Do not share exploits with unauthorized parties
   - Maintain confidentiality agreements
